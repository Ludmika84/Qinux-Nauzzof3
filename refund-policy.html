Refund & returns policy

Last updated: 19 July 2026

This Refund & Returns Policy explains how cancellations, returns and refunds work for Qinux Nauzzof purchases started from this UK promotional website operated by ECOMM MOVADGENCY SL.

It summarises key rights under the Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013 (“CCRs”) and the Consumer Rights Act 2015 (“CRA”). It does not replace the selling retailer’s checkout terms. Where there is a conflict, mandatory UK consumer law prevails.

1. Who handles your order and refund

Orders placed after clicking through from this site are usually completed with an external retailer. That retailer is responsible for:

  • taking payment and confirming your order
  • delivery and shipping
  • processing cancellations, returns and refunds
  • handling faulty-goods claims under the CRA

We can help direct your enquiry via Contact us, but the retailer remains the primary point of contact shown on your order confirmation.

2. Your statutory cooling-off right (distance sales)

If you buy goods as a consumer by distance contract (online), the CCRs generally give you a right to cancel within 14 days without giving a reason.

  • The cancellation period usually ends 14 days after the day you (or someone you nominate) receive the goods
  • If goods are delivered in multiple lots, the period usually ends 14 days after the day you receive the last lot
  • You should inform the retailer of your decision to cancel within that period (email is normally sufficient if it clearly identifies the order)

After cancelling, you must normally return the goods without undue delay and not later than 14 days after telling the retailer you are cancelling, unless the retailer offers to collect them.

3. Refunds after cooling-off cancellation

For a valid CCR cancellation, the retailer must normally refund:

  • the price paid for the goods; and
  • standard delivery costs (but not usually upgraded delivery you chose)

Refunds should be made without undue delay and within 14 days of the earlier of receiving the goods back or evidence you have sent them back (or, if no goods were supplied, within 14 days of being informed of cancellation). Refunds are usually made using the same payment method.

The retailer may deduct an amount if the value of the goods is diminished because of handling beyond what is necessary to establish their nature, characteristics and functioning.

4. Who pays return shipping on cooling-off returns

Unless the retailer stated otherwise before purchase, you may be responsible for the direct cost of returning goods under the cooling-off right. Check your order confirmation and the retailer’s terms.

5. Promotional 14-day money-back guarantee

Where a promotional money-back guarantee is advertised, it may offer additional commercial protection alongside (not instead of) your statutory rights. Eligibility may require unused product and original packaging — but any promotional conditions cannot take away CRA/CCR rights that the law gives you.

6. Faulty, misdescribed or not as described goods (CRA 2015)

Under the Consumer Rights Act 2015, goods must be:

  • of satisfactory quality
  • fit for a particular purpose made known to the retailer
  • as described

Key remedies typically include:

  • Short-term right to reject — generally within 30 days of delivery/ownership for faulty goods, for a full refund
  • Repair or replacement — if the short-term reject period has passed or you choose these remedies
  • Price reduction or final right to reject — if repair/replacement is not possible, fails, or is not provided in a reasonable time without significant inconvenience

If goods are faulty, you should not be left out of pocket for returning them where the CRA requires the trader to bear those costs.

7. Exceptions to cooling-off (where permitted by law)

The CCR cooling-off right may not apply in limited cases set out in the Regulations (for example sealed goods unsealed after delivery that are not suitable for return due to health protection or hygiene, where that exception lawfully applies). The retailer must tell you clearly before purchase if a cancellation exception applies.

8. Damaged, lost or delayed parcels

Report non-delivery, delay or transit damage to the retailer promptly, ideally with photos and your order number. If you purchased optional parcel protection, cover follows the retailer’s protection terms. Your CRA rights for goods that arrive damaged remain available.

9. How to start a return or cancellation

  1. Check your order confirmation for the retailer’s returns email/address
  2. State that you wish to cancel/return, include your order number, name and contact details
  3. Follow the return instructions and keep proof of postage
  4. If you cannot reach the retailer, contact us at support@qinux-nauzzof.co.uk and we will help escalate

10. Chargebacks and payment disputes

Please contact the retailer (and us if needed) before raising a card chargeback, so the issue can usually be resolved faster. This does not limit any rights you may have against your payment provider.

11. Your statutory rights

Nothing in this policy excludes or limits your mandatory rights under the Consumer Rights Act 2015, the Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013, or other applicable UK consumer law. If any commercial term conflicts with those rights, the statutory right applies.
