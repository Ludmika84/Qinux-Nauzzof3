Cookie policy

Last updated: 19 July 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how ECOMM MOVADGENCY SL (“we”, “us”, “our”) uses cookies and similar storage and access technologies on this Qinux Nauzzof website for visitors in the United Kingdom.

This policy should be read together with our Privacy policy. It is designed to meet the requirements of the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 (PECR), UK GDPR, and related updates under the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025.

1. What are cookies and similar technologies?

Cookies are small files placed on your device. Similar technologies include local storage, pixels, tags and scripts that store or access information on your terminal equipment. UK law regulates these technologies based on what they do, not only whether they are called “cookies”.

2. Our legal approach

Under PECR Regulation 6, we must:

  • give you clear and comprehensive information about our purposes; and
  • obtain your consent before storing or accessing non-essential information on your device, unless an exemption applies

Consent must be freely given, specific, informed and unambiguous, and given by a clear positive action. Continuing to browse alone is not valid consent. “Accept” and “Decline” choices on our banner are presented equally.

Strictly necessary technologies (needed to provide a service you request, for example security or storing your cookie choice) may be used without consent.

Following the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025, certain limited technologies used solely for statistical purposes, or to adapt appearance/functionality to preferences, may be exempt from consent in defined circumstances. Where we rely on such an exemption, we will still provide clear information and a simple free way to object.

3. How we use cookies on this site

  • Strictly necessary / functional — keep the site working securely and remember essential choices
  • Preference — store your cookie consent decision
  • Analytics — understand traffic and improve content (used only with consent, unless a lawful PECR statistical exemption applies)
  • Advertising / affiliate measurement — measure and attribute campaigns (for example Taboola or offer partners) only with consent where required

5. Consent and your choices

When you first visit, our cookie banner lets you Accept or Decline non-essential cookies. Until you accept, we aim not to set non-essential advertising or analytics technologies.

You can change your mind at any time by:

  • using Manage cookie preferences in the website footer; or
  • clearing this site’s cookies/local storage in your browser (the banner will appear again); or
  • using your browser settings to block or delete cookies; or
  • contacting us via the Contact page

Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal. Strictly necessary technologies may still operate.

6. Managing cookies in your browser

Most browsers let you refuse or delete cookies. Blocking all cookies may affect site functionality. Guidance is available from your browser provider and from the ICO: https://ico.org.uk/for-the-public/online/cookies/.

7. Third-party technologies

If you arrive via an advertising network or continue to an external checkout, those third parties may set their own cookies under their policies. We do not control those technologies once you leave our site.

8. Updates

We may update this Cookie Policy to reflect technology, legal or operational changes. The “Last updated” date will change when we do.
